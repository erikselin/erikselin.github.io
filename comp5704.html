Project Title: XRT 0.1.0 - A Language-Agnostic MapReduce Runtime for Shared-Memory Systems

Name: Erik Selin

Project Outline: MapReduce is a restrictive programming model for writing data processing programs without the complexity of parallel programming. Motivated by BSP, MPI and other restrictive programming models, MapReduce was introduced by Google and popularized by Hadoop MapReduce. Hadoop MapReduce operates on networked commodity hardware but following its success multiple MapReduce runtimes have been developed for additional environments like GPUs, FGPAs, Coprocessors and shared-memory systems. Shared-memory systems are becoming increasingly adapt at data processing due to higher core-counts, greater memory capacity and improved secondary storage. Current MapReduce runtimes for shared-memory systems achieve high performance but are overly specialized and forces programmers to write C++ which is not realistic for most engineering teams. XRT is a novel MapReduce runtime for shared-memory systems inspired by Hadoop streaming and built from the ground up to support language-agnostic MapReduce. Developed in Go, XRT offers a restrictive programming model focused on operational simplicity, cross platform compatibility and industry readiness without sacrificing performance. The goals, concepts and internals of XRT version 0.1.0 will be introduced together with a comparative benchmark.
