Project Title: XRT 0.1.0 - A Language-Agnostic Map-Reduce Runtime for Shared-Memory Systems

Name: Erik Selin

Project Outline: Map-reduce is a restrictive programming model for writing data processing programs without the complexity of parallel programming. Motivated by BSP, MPI and other restrictive programming models, map-reduce was introduced by Google and popularized by Hadoop MapReduce. Hadoop MapReduce operates on networked commodity hardware but following its success multiple map-reduce runtimes have been developed for additional environments like GPUs, FGPAs, Coprocessors and shared-memory systems. Shared-memory systems are becoming increasingly adapt at data processing due to higher core-counts, greater memory capacity and improved secondary storage. Current map-reduce runtimes for shared-memory systems achieve high performance but are overly specialized and forces programmers to write C++ which is not realistic for most engineering teams. XRT is a novel map-reduce runtime for shared-memory systems inspired by Hadoop streaming and built from the ground up to support language-agnostic map-reduce. Developed in Go, XRT offers a restrictive map-reduce programming model focused on operational simplicity, cross platform compatibility and industry readiness without sacrificing performance. The goals, concepts and internals of XRT version 0.1.0 will be introduced together with a comparative benchmark.
